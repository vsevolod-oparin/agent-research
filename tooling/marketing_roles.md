# Research Agent Roles — Top 10

Practical research agents for market research, competitive intelligence, product research, and applied investigation. Focused on actionable output, not academic ceremony.

---

### 1. Research Strategist
Designs the research plan: defines the question, picks methodology, sets scope and success criteria. Decides whether the task needs a quick scan, deep competitive analysis, or full market study. Produces a research brief before anything else starts.

### 2. Source Hunter
Finds and retrieves relevant information across all source types — industry reports, competitor sites, patent filings, news, social media, forums, academic papers, government data. Builds search strategies, handles snowballing, deduplicates. The workhorse of information gathering.

### 3. Data Extractor
Pulls structured facts from unstructured sources into clean tables. Extracts: pricing, features, market size numbers, user demographics, key claims, timelines, methodologies. Handles messy formats (PDFs, web pages, earnings calls). Outputs standardized comparison matrices.

### 4. Insight Synthesizer
The core analytical role. Connects dots across sources — identifies patterns, contradictions, trends, and causal relationships. Produces thematic summaries, competitive landscapes, opportunity maps. Distinguishes signal from noise. Answers "so what?" for every finding.

### 5. Critical Reviewer
Stress-tests findings and conclusions. Hunts for counter-evidence, challenges assumptions, flags weak data, identifies logical gaps. Checks whether claims are supported by evidence or just correlation. Prevents confirmation bias and overconfident recommendations.

### 6. Market Analyst
Specializes in market sizing, segmentation, trend forecasting, and competitive positioning. Analyzes TAM/SAM/SOM, growth rates, market share dynamics, pricing strategies, and go-to-market patterns. Reads financial reports, analyst coverage, and industry benchmarks.

### 7. User/Customer Researcher
Focuses on the human side — user needs, pain points, behaviors, and preferences. Designs survey questions, interprets interview transcripts, analyzes sentiment and feedback data. Builds personas, journey maps, and jobs-to-be-done frameworks. Bridges qualitative insights with quantitative validation.

### 8. Competitive Intelligence Analyst
Deep-dives on specific competitors or alternative solutions. Tracks product launches, feature changes, hiring patterns, funding rounds, patent filings, partnership announcements. Builds competitive matrices and identifies strategic moves. Monitors positioning shifts over time.

### 9. Research Writer
Turns raw analysis into clear, persuasive deliverables — executive summaries, investment memos, market briefs, strategy decks, blog posts. Adapts depth and tone to the audience (C-suite vs. product team vs. investors). Leads with the insight, not the methodology.

### 10. Source Validator
Checks credibility of sources and claims. Verifies data provenance, identifies outdated numbers, cross-references statistics against primary sources. Flags biased reports (vendor-sponsored research, cherry-picked data), retracted studies, and unreliable outlets. The quality gate before anything gets cited.

---

## How They Compose

| Task | Agents needed |
|------|---------------|
| Quick market scan | Source Hunter + Insight Synthesizer |
| Competitive landscape | Source Hunter + Competitive Intelligence Analyst + Market Analyst |
| Full market study | All 10 |
| User research project | User/Customer Researcher + Insight Synthesizer + Research Writer |
| Due diligence | Source Hunter + Data Extractor + Source Validator + Critical Reviewer |
| Trend report | Source Hunter + Market Analyst + Insight Synthesizer + Research Writer |
